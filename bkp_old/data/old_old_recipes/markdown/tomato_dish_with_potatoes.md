# Tomato Dish with Potatoes (דביח תטמטם)

## Cultural Background
The "Tomato Dish with Potatoes" (*dbih tmtm* - דביח תטמטם) is a cherished part of Tunisian Jewish cuisine, particularly from the Djerba community. This hearty stew, rich in flavor and tradition, showcases the resourcefulness of the community by utilizing simple ingredients like tomatoes and potatoes, while its slow-cooked preparation honors the Sabbath practice of enjoying nourishing meals with family.

## Ingredient List
- **Onions** (2 large, thinly sliced)  
  *Essential for building flavor.*
  
- **Olive Oil** (1/4 cup)  
  *Retained from tradition; provides richness.*
  
- **Tomato Paste (Resek)** (3 tablespoons)  
  *Adds depth and thickness to the stew.*
  
- **Fresh Tomatoes** (4 cups, chopped)  
  *Provides freshness and acidity; use ripe tomatoes for best flavor.*
  
- **Potatoes (Tfuchai Adama)** (4 medium, peeled and cubed)  
  *The main body of the dish.*
  
- **Vegetable Stock** (4 cups)  
  *Substitutes animal broth and provides umami flavor.*
  
- **Cumin** (1 teaspoon)  
  *A hallmark of Tunisian spice blends.*
  
- **Coriander** (1 teaspoon)  
  *Enhances the aromatic profile.*
  
- **Paprika** (1 teaspoon)  
  *Adds warmth and color.*
  
- **Turmeric** (1/2 teaspoon)  
  *Imparts earthiness and vibrant hue.*
  
- **Optional: Harissa** (1 teaspoon)  
  *For heat; adjust to taste.*
  
- **Salt and Pepper** (to taste)  
  *Essential for balancing flavors.*
  
- **Chickpeas** (1 can, drained and rinsed) *(optional)*  
  *For additional protein and texture.*

## Step-by-Step Instructions
1. **Prepare the Base:** In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the sliced onions and sauté for about 10-15 minutes until they become soft and caramelized, stirring occasionally.

2. **Add the Spices:** Stir in the cumin, coriander, paprika, turmeric, and optional harissa. Cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the spices are fragrant.

3. **Incorporate Tomato Paste and Fresh Tomatoes:** Add the tomato paste and chopped fresh tomatoes to the pot, stirring well to combine. Allow it to cook for about 5 minutes until the tomatoes start breaking down.

4. **Add Potatoes and Stock:** Add the cubed potatoes and pour in the vegetable stock. Stir well to ensure all ingredients are evenly distributed.

5. **Simmer the Stew:** Bring the mixture to a gentle boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover the pot and let it simmer for about 40-50 minutes, or until the potatoes are tender and the flavors meld together. Stir occasionally and add more stock or water if needed.

6. **Add Chickpeas (Optional):** If using, add the drained chickpeas during the last 10 minutes of cooking. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

7. **Final Touches:** Once cooked, taste and adjust the seasoning as needed. If desired, garnish with fresh herbs like cilantro or parsley before serving.

## Serving Suggestions
Serve the Tomato Dish with Potatoes warm, either on its own or accompanied by vegan Challah bread, warm pita, or couscous. This dish is perfect for communal gatherings, especially on *Leil Shabbat* (שבת לי"ל), honoring its traditional roots.

## Cultural Context and Family Notes
This recipe is a treasured family tradition from the Silver-Cohen-Trabelsi lineage, often prepared for Sabbath meals. The lengthy cooking time reflects a commitment to flavor and community, making it a staple for gatherings. Its rich aroma and comforting texture evoke nostalgia and the warmth of family heritage, ensuring that this culinary legacy continues to be celebrated across generations.

## Preparation Time and Difficulty Level
- **Preparation Time:** 15 minutes  
- **Cooking Time:** 50 minutes  
- **Total Time:** 1 hour 5 minutes  
- **Difficulty Level:** Easy

## Yield
- **Serves:** 6-8 people

This vegan adaptation of *dbih tmtm* maintains the authenticity and heart of the original Silver-Cohen-Trabelsi family recipe while ensuring it is accessible to all dietary preferences. Enjoy this flavorful stew as a nourishing meal that honors tradition and family!
