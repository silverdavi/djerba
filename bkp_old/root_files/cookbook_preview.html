Mahmessa

Ingredients

  • 1 small onion
  • 1 cup of ptitim (Israeli couscous)
  • 1 tablespoon sweet paprika
  • Black pepper to taste
  • 1 chopped tomato
  • Vegetables of your choice
  • Water (1 and 1/4 cups for every cup of ptitim)

Preparation

  1. For dry Mahmessa: Sauté a small onion until translucent.
  2. Add the ptitim (Mahmessa) and continue to sauté for a few more minutes. Then, add water according to the instructions: 1 and 1/4 cups of water for every cup of ptitim.
  3. For Mahmessa with sauce: Sauté a small onion until it starts to brown. Add 1 tablespoon of sweet paprika and sauté for a few seconds.
  4. Add the chopped tomato and any vegetables you desire. Season with salt, black pepper, and add boiling water.
  5. Stir in the ptitim.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat, cover partially, and simmer until cooked through.

Preparation

Sufganiyot

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 packet (7g) dry yeast
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup warm milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • Oil for frying
  • Powdered sugar for dusting
  • Jam or chocolate filling

Preparation

  1. In a large mixing bowl, combine the flour, sugar, yeast, and salt.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the warm milk, eggs, and melted butter.
  3. Pour the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients and mix until a dough forms.
  4. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  5. Place the dough in a greased bowl, cover with a cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  6. Once the dough has risen, punch it down and roll it out to about 1/2 inch thickness.
  7. Cut out circles using a cookie cutter or glass, and let them rise for another 30 minutes.
  8. Heat oil in a deep pan to 350°F (175°C).
  9. Fry the dough circles in batches until golden brown on both sides, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  10. Remove the sufganiyot from the oil and drain on paper towels.
  11. Once cooled, fill with jam or chocolate and dust with powdered sugar before serving.

Kta'a Soup (Torn Dough Soup)

Ingredients

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3-4 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 2-3 ripe tomatoes, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon sweet paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1.5 liters water or vegetable broth
  • Juice of half a lemon
  • Handful chopped cilantro (optional)

Preparation

  1. Prepare the dough: Mix flour and salt in a bowl. Gradually add water and knead until a uniform and flexible dough forms. Cover and let rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Prepare the soup base: Heat olive oil in a large pot. Sauté onion and garlic until golden.
  3. Add crushed tomatoes and tomato paste, cook for a few minutes.
  4. Season with cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  5. Add water or broth, bring to a boil, and simmer on low heat for about 10 minutes.
  6. Add the dough: Tear small pieces of the dough and drop them into the boiling soup.
  7. Cook for another 10 minutes until the dough is cooked through and soft.
  8. Finish: Stir in lemon juice and cilantro. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  9. Serve warm.
