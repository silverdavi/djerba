*   **Arabic Root:** The Arabic term associated with "שמיד" is **شْميد** (Shmid) or **شْمَيْد** (Shmayd), which appears to be a regional or dialectal variant of **بْسِيسَة** (Bsisa).
*   **Literal Meaning:** The root ب-س-س (b-s-s) in Arabic relates to "mixing," "grinding," or "powdering." Bsisa is a powder made from roasted grains and legumes. The meaning of شْميد is less clear, but given its association with Bsisa, it likely refers to a similar concept related to ground or mixed ingredients.
*   **Alternate Spellings/Names:**
